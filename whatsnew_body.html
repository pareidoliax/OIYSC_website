Normal meeting schedule:
Themes and fund raising projects. Some special events
Dinner meetings are on the fourth Monday of the month  and will be in Room 220 of  the Metro Y  in most cases.  October is when we focus on membership as we start a new club year. 
Club members staff  the Fair  World Y Boutique, in the lobby of the Metro Y.		 The Peace Action conference for youth is held at a different Y camp each year

The Central Canada Regional Convention is normally held in October
Executive/business and planning meetings are on the second Monday at the Y.  Club members and visitors are welcome to attend.  December is the busiest month of the year with Christmas Tree sales in front of the Metro Y, 180 Argyle Ave and the Christmas Bazaar the first weekend of the month.
 
 

 

 The annual Christmas Pot Luck is open to members, family and friends of Y FOCUS 
Special activities such as socials and service/fund raising projects are held on other Mondays or weekends.  February  is Time of Fast month when club members give up some meals and contribute the money they save to support YMCA projects around the world. Some members also fast for 24 hours, getting sponsors for each hour fasted.
Committee meetings are suggested for the first Monday, but are at the discretion of each committee and its
members schedules. 		 We hold an annual Easter Plant and Used Book sale in the Y Lobby. The Kingston Y Service club usually visits us in April.
April and May are important months for selling our Fair Trade Soccer balls. In early  May there is  the celebration  of Fair Trade Weeks with  a Fair  Trade  Fair at the Metro Y.
June is the end of our club year and we celebrate with a barbecque and swim at a members home. We have the opportunity of helping the Y with cleanups at the camps or as volunteers for the fund raising Human Race
In odd numbered years, the Canadian clubs have an Area convention. In 2007 it will be hosted by  the Ottawa club. Our International Convention is held in even numbered  years during July. In 2008 it is in Denmark
