The Freetown Y Service club with the support of Y FOCUS, is implementing a training program for unemployed young adults, where they learn about indigenous products they can make and receive basic business training so that they can form co-operatives to provide them with jobs and incomes. 56 young people in 8 communities are being trained during the first year of the program and will be helped to set up their own business usually as a co-operative in groups of 4-5 youth.

 

There is also an orphanage called Jonathan’s House supported by the Y Service club and the YMCA in Freetown. The home is committed to finding kids on the streets and providing food, clothing, shelter, education and medical treatment. The children’s fees and uniforms for school are paid for and the YMCA in Freetown provides administrative support and oversight. There are 62 children in the program, boys and girls of all ages with a budget of $35 000 per year.

 

Sierra Leone is located in the southwestern part of West Africa and is about the size of New Brunswick, 72 000 square kilometres. The population is estimated to be just over 4 million but exact numbers are difficult to state due to the displacement and death of so many people during the civil war. Sierra Leone is bound by Guinea to the north and northeast, Liberia to the southeast and the Atlantic Ocean to the south and southwest.

Sierra Leone has a tropical climate with a wet season from May to October and a dry season from November to April. Average annual precipitation varies from 5,080 mm (200 inches) along the coast and decreases inland towards the north to 2,160 mm (86 inches). The prevailing winds are the SW Monsoons during the wet season and the northeastern Harmattan, a dust laden wind from the Sahara Desert, during the dry season. Average temperature ranges in Freetown are from 21 degrees Celsius (73 degrees Fahrenheit) to 31 degrees Celsius (88 degrees Fahrenheit) all year.

The official language is English, although it is only used for government, commerce and media purposes. The principal native languages are Krio which is a form of pidgin English mixed with African words as well as Mende and Temne. Many individuals speak and understand two or more indigenous languages.

 

 

 

Republic of Sierra Leone at a glance.

President: Ahmad Tejan Kabbah (1998)

Land area: 27,653 sq mi (71,621 sq km); total area: 27,699 sq mi (71,740 sq km)

Population (2006 est.): 6,005,250 (growth rate: 2.3%); birth rate: 45.8/1000; infant mortality rate: 160.4/1000; life expectancy: 40.2; density per sq mi: 217

Capital and largest city: Freetown, 1,051,000

Monetary unit: Leone

Languages: English (official), Mende (southern vernacular), Temne (northern vernacular), Krio (lingua franca)

Ethinicity/race: 20 native African tribes 90% (Temne 30%, Mende 30%, other 30%); Creole (Krio) 10%; refugees from Liberia's recent civil war, small numbers of Europeans, Lebanese, Pakistanis, and Indians

Religions: Islam 60%, indigenous 30%, Christian 10%

Literacy rate: 31% (1995 est.)

 

The Flag:

The Sierra Leone flag is shown with the colours of green for the vegetation, white for the sandy beaches and blue for the waters of the Atlantic Ocean. Sierra Leonians are very proud of their national team called the Stars and it has been a perennial contender for the African championships.
