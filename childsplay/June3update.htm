"LET THE CHILDREN PLAY" Update. June 3rd, 2006.

There was a great article by Richard Starnes in the Ottawa Citizen of June 3rd, about the importance of soccer in promoting peace and giving children all over the world the opportunity to have some joy and purpose in their lives. Here’s the link to the article:

http://www.canada.com/ottawacitizen/news/observer/story.html?id=1992373e-ddff-4186-befd-ec78ffdad886

In Ottawa, we are inviting individuals and groups to do something tangible by collecting and donating used soccer balls and equipment, as well as cash, through a program called "Let the Children Play" sponsored by the Ottawa Y Service club, Y FOCUS. We are passing on all donations to 10 Canadian based organizations who work with children in 15 different countries. Some of these agencies are based in the Ottawa area with projects such as a vocational training program for orphans in Uganda, an orphanage in Nepal, a youth run bakery in El Salvador and an SOS Children’s Village in Brazil. The full list of agencies and projects is available on our web site at www.ncf.ca/yfocus/childsplay

To date we have the following results to report, from our campaign:

The 3rd annual Fair Trade-No Sweat Soccer Tournament, Playfair 2006, will be held Sunday, June 18th at Brewer Park and the teams will be collecting donations. The public is invited to come down and bring their contributions to the event.

1150 pairs of soccer shoes donated by Elgin Sports. The children’s sizes will be donated to local organizations working with needy children.

12 sets of team uniforms with soccer balls for several schools, from the Almonte District Soccer League.

The Ottawa Royals Soccer Club will be asking their players and families to bring donations to their annual Fun Day, August 26th.

St Anthony’s Soccer Club has agreed to set up a donations box at their club house during the World Cup.

There will be drop-off boxes at the following locations during the World Cup, starting June 9th: the Metro, Carlingwood, Orleans, Merivale and Kanata Y branches ; all Bridgehead locations; Ten Thousand Villages in Westboro and other businesses, schools and churches are being approached to participate.

There are donation baskets, posters and information cards available for placing in your favourite sports bar so that patrons viewing World Cup games can help out.

For more information, to donate or to volunteer your help, contact Dave Hall at dfhall@magma.ca
