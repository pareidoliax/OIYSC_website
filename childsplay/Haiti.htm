Haiti .

Here is some information about the country and the particular projects you will be helping with your donations.

The agency being supported is ACCESO INTERNATIONAL based in Ottawa. www.accesointernational.ca

ACCESO International supports a homework centre in Verettes and a primary school in Gonaïves. The homework centre in Verettes offers literacy support for at-risk, impoverished and orphaned children. Activities include tutoring sessions, basic literacy and pre-school classes, as well as summer camps. The intention is to enable these children to grow as persons who are free, responsible, builders of peace and committed to changing their world so that they will be able to actively shape their own lives and to experience all the rights to which they are entitled. At the primary school in Gonaïves, ACCESO International is providing educational supplies and a much needed meal program for over 700 children who attend daily classes in three different shifts. ACCESO International is assisting this school in its efforts to rebuild after it was severely damaged by a hurricane in 2004.

 

 

The Flag:

COUNTRY DESCRIPTION: Haiti is one of the least developed and least stable countries in the Western Hemisphere. The availability of consumer goods and services is barely adequate in the capital, Port-au-Prince, but other parts of the country experience chronic shortages. Most consumer products are imported and expensive. Some tourism facilities in the large cities and resort areas are satisfactory, but many are rudimentary at best, and are difficult to find in most rural areas and small towns

 

Republic of Haiti at a glance:

National name: République d'Haïti

President: Boniface Alexandre (interim) (2004)

Prime Minister: Gérard Latortue (interim) (2004)

Land area: 10,641 sq mi (27,560 sq km); total area: 10,714 sq mi (27,750 sq km)

Population (2006 est.): 8,308,504 (growth rate: 2.3%); birth rate: 36.4/1000; infant mortality rate: 71.7/1000; life expectancy: 53.2; density per sq mi: 781

Capital and largest city (2003 est.): Port-au-Prince, 1,764,000 (metro. area), 1,119,000 (city proper)

Monetary unit: Gourde

Languages: Creole and French (both official)

Ethnicity/race: black 95%, mulatto and white 5%

Religions: Roman Catholic 80%, Protestant 16% (Baptist 10%, Pentecostal 4%, Adventist 1%, other 1%), other 3%, none 1%. Note: roughly half the population practices Vaudou

Literacy rate: 53% (2003 est.)
