International Programs

Here is a list of programs and web sites  in Canada and around the world where groups and individuals can get information and can get directly involved. We welcome suggestions of sites you have found useful.

 

Y's Men International.    http://www.ysmen.org/

At our international web site, you will find additional sources of information from around the world on the international theme "Say No To Violence" plus news about the activities of Y Service clubs in other countries.
 

The Hunger Site     http://www.hungersite.com/   

At this web site you can make donations for clearing land mines, helping children in need and for providing food with just the click of a mouse and all donations are paid for by the site sponsors. Visit every day for best results.
 
 

Amnesty International   http://www.amnesty.org/ & http://www.amnesty.ca/

Amnesty International, interested in Human Rights. Agood web site with letter writing campaign information.Their national headquarters is located in Ottawa, phone number is (613) 744?7667.

 

Canadian Land Mines foundation     http://www.canadianlandmine.com/

Information on how to adopt an area for clearing the land mines.

 

Clear Land Mines      http://www.clearlandmines.com/

More info on land mines and with an easy campaign to support land mine elimination.

 

Maquila Solidarity Network  http://www.maquilasolidarity.org/

Excellent site!Good information about sweat shop labour and excellent resources to join in with their campaign against it.

 

UNICEF         http://unicef.org/

General information on children's problems around the globe.

 

Greenpeace    http://www.greenpeace.org/ & http:// www.greenpeace.ca/

Good site for environmental issues as well as disarmament.

 

Doctors Without Borders      http://www.msf.org/

Good site for information on projects undertaken by Medecins Sans Frontieres.In Canada visit

                                                                            http://www.msf.ca

Peace Brigades International http://www.igc.apc.org/pbi/index.html

Organization that promotes and takes part in non?violent peacekeeping activities around the world.

Hague Appeal for Peace Secretariat.        http://www.haguepeace.org/
The co-ordinator for the Hague Appeal's Campaign for Peace Educations. The Hague Conference was held in 1999 and there are many initiatives from that conference that deserve your support.

Canadian Organizations


Canadian Council for International Co-operation    http://www.web.net/ccic-ccci
The council co-ordinates initiatives of various NGOs involved with development and peace.

 

The Canadian Ecumenical Jubilee Initiative                        http://www.ceji?iocj.org/English/index.html

Group of churches concerned with debt relief, climate change, etc. Excellent information site. Also contains information for promotional materials and work shops.

 

YWCA Canada           http://www.ywcacanada.ca/

YWCA Week Without Violence, annually in October; participating organizations set up displays and arrange information sessions.If you serve a family Y or YMCA-YWCA in North America, check with your Y. http://www.7wv.com/

 

Youth Canada Association YOUCan         http://www.youcan.ca/

Regional groups of students aged 12-25 are trained in conflict resolution techniques, pass them on, and establish school and community groups that nurture the ideal of a peaceful community. They have skilled trainers and training resources to share and would like to communicate with other youth groups with the same ideals.

 

MATCH International Centre   http://www.web.ca/~matchint

MATCH International Centre is a non-profit, non-governmental development agency that concentrates its support on women through overseas project funding and education in Canada.

 

Children’s Aid Societies      http://www.casoc.com/

Child abuse and neglect month in October

 

Warchild                     http://www.warchild.ca/

Good site on child soldiers and other issues affecting children. 

 

Free The Children      http://www.freethechildren.org/

Information on child labour, good options for younger kids and what they can do to help the problem of child labour.

 

Oxfam Canada           http://www.ofxam.org/ &http://www.oxfam.ca/

Oxfam International, interested in issues of education, sweat shops, human rights etc...Could be a very goodorganization to look at supporting. Their national headquarters is in Ottawa phone # is (613) 237-5236.
