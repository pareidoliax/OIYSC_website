CANADIAN FRIENDS OF SIERRA LEONE
 

Fund raising event.                      Sierra Leone Dinner and Auction.
 

Location: Lakeside Gardens, Brittania Park.                    Date: Friday, April 5th 2002.

Over 200 people attended this event, emceed by Cecilia Haslett , of the Sierra Leone Descendants Union of Ottawa, and welcomed by the Honourable Flora MacDonald  Dinner was a wonderful buffet, provided  by Dave Smith and volunteer young people from several churches assisted in waiting on the tables. Before and during the meal, guests were able to view over 120  silent auction items and enjoy background music from D.J.  Robert Kruikemeijer, a Sierra Leone born Canadian who donated his services.
    After dinner young members of the SLDU presented some dance numbers to Sierra Leone music and Noumousso Kone, SLDU President, expressed thanks to all who helped with the event. Dave Smith carried on the Live Auction and managed to extract a few thousand dollars from a rather parsimonious crowd. Those who were still standing after the auction stayed to dance until the end. With a profit of between $10 000 and $11 000, the event was a success but it was even more of a success  in terms of building a feeling of community between the various groups who came together to carry out the event.

Much thanks goes out to members of the constituent groups of CFSL, Ottawa Monthly Meeting, Bells Corners United Church, Northwestern United Church, Ottawa Unitarian Church, Sierra Leone Descendants Union, Y FOCUS - Ottawa,  Peacefund Canada and the Canadian Friends Service Committee.

Instructions for the obtaining of items for auction.

Using the attached letter of introduction, approach businesses with whom you have a good relationship and ask for donations for silent (value under $100) or live auction (value over $100). Keep a record of the donor, address, contact person and phone number with a description of the item donated including estimated value. Pass the items on to Sandy Higgs - Nesbitt or Dave Hall, preferably in an envelope or plastic bag for protection.
