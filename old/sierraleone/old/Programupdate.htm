BUILDING A CULTURE OF PEACE IN SIERRA LEONE

 
The Canadian Friends of Sierra Leone comprises several groups based in Ottawa. These are Peacefund Canada, the lead agency, Canadian Friends Service Committee (Quakers), Ottawa Monthly Meeting, Sierra Leone Descendants Union of Ottawa, Y FOCUS-Ottawa, Northwestern United Church, Ottawa Unitarian Church, Bells Corners United Church. We work with three established and reputable NGO's in Sierra Leone, the Sierra Leone Christian Council of Churches, the Network Movement for Justice and Development and the Freetown Y Service Club with the Sierra Leone YMCA.

Thanks to major funding from the CIDA Peacebuilding Unit and the Canadian Auto Workers Union, we have been able to fund projects aimed at building the peace, providing emergency assistance and supporting small business through micro credit. The project started in January 2001 and has the following components:

Project objectives.

· improve the lives of community members in Sierra Leone by implementing a holistic program that addresses their humanitarian, economic development and security needs.

· increase awareness of community conditions, needs, attitudes, feasible strategies and options to be developed towards building peace from the community level

· build capacity within communities and within and among NGOs to plan and carry out activities and programmes for the well-being of all community members.
 

The main activities.

· provide emergency assistance (food and non-food items) to those most in need, who have difficulty accessing other sources of assistance

· begin a sustained process of reconciliation, trauma healing, and human rights sensitization at the community level

· develop and implement a community-level micro-projects fund providing loans, especially to young people and women who wish to set up their own businesses. Giving grants to communities for development projects in agriculture, water and sanitation services, medical resources, education and skills training.

· co-operate with other providers of humanitarian assistance, including sharing of information insights, and capacities

· strengthen partnership management skills among the partner agencies of the Sierra Leone Partners and the Canadian Friends of Sierra Leone.
 

Program Update.

In July of 2002, David Bruer, Executive Director of Peacefund Canada, and David Hall of Y FOCUS-Ottawa, representing Canadian Friends of Sierra Leone (CFSL), visited several projects around Sierra Leone. The purpose of the trip was to gather information, firstly for a report to CIDA due in October and secondly for promotion of the project in future fund raising as we seek to expand the program, building on the good success of the first two years. A full report on the visit and the progress in the Culture of Peace program is now available.
 

The three S.L. Partners have identified 15 chiefdoms and communities which they consider most damaged by the war and with high numbers of the most vulnerable people, such as widows and single mothers, displaced persons, returning ex-combatants (especially young people) and farmers coming back from refugee camps in Guinea and Liberia. In the first 18 months they focused on

a) providing emergency aid such as seeds, tools and household utensils to displaced persons and returning farmers.
b) training of over 400 peace educators who are now active in their communities in settling family and land disputes, in helping ex-combattants return to their communities and in trauma healing.
c) providing small loans to individuals and community groups to start small enterprises and generate income.

Building on the success of the present initiatives, CFSL and its Sierra Leone Partners have now developed proposals for the continuation and expansion of the work, to take advantage of the network of trained people now in place. There will be an added focus on rebuilding primary education in the country since this has been identified as the next priority in the maintenance of a lasting peace.
