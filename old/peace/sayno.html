Say No To Violence - Build a Culture of Peace

 

A program of Y Service Clubs in Canada. 

 

In 1989, at the Area Convention held in Ottawa, Y Service clubs in Canada adopted as a theme for their service work “Youth at Risk”. Many clubs still use this theme as a focus for their work. In 2001, Canadian clubs have expanded the original theme to reflect the choice of Say No To Violence as an international theme to be used by all Y Service clubs in all 70 countries. This decision was made by the International Council in 2000 and in 2001 clubs in the Central Canada Region agreed to add the phrase Build a Culture of Peace to support the U.N. Decade for Non-violence and a Culture of Peace for Children.

 

All Canadian Y Service clubs are urged to include the principles of non-violence and peace building in all aspects of their club life, raising awareness of the issues within their club, YMCA and community and encouraging people to get involved in some form of direct action. Over the next five years, clubs will be encouraged to explore different themes and consider new initiatives in their service work that relate to the overall thrust of the program.

 

This web site provides information and specific suggestions for action by Y Service clubs, YMCAs and other interested groups. We have included 

- devotional material as a basis for reflection, discussion and action by our members.

- lists of information sources, especially web sites, on issues of non-violence and peace building plus suggested speakers, videos, simulation games and 

  discussion papers.

- summaries of interesting and innovative projects of national and international organizations which clubs, groups and individuals can support.

 

The Y Service Clubs of Canada have also developed a free Information Kit to include the information on this site plus additional ideas. We will also develop materials for displays for use in YMCAs and churches and look at other ways of promoting the U.N. Decade in our communities. A major thrust will be to work in partnership with our local YMCAs, YMCA Canada and other interested groups with similar goals.
