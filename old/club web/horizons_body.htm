Y FOCUS- Ottawa
International Outreach Projects

As part of an international movement, Y FOCUS has a strong interest in what goes on around the world. Our International theme is "Build a Culture of Peace - Say No to Violence" 

a) International development. 
As our way of supporting the international development work of the YMCA world-wide, our club raises funds through voluntary contributions and a 24 Hour Fast held in February, the month when all clubs in the international movement participate in the Time of Fast program. We encourage members to fast and obtain sponsors or at least sponsor another member. The February dinner meeting has an international development theme and at least two other dinners are devoted to international topics as part of our attempt to educate our members about international issues. Through Time of Fast we have directly supported the partnership of the Ottawa-Carleton Y with the Togo YMCA. 

b) The newest project is the importation and sale of Fair Trade Sports Balls partly to raise funds for our projects in war-torn countries but also to promote the principles of Fair Trade and draw attention to the evil of child labour in industries such as hand stitched sports balls. The Fair World Sports project  is operated on behalf  of all Y Service clubs  in Canada with sales made throughout Canada and into  the U.S. and  other countries. A new project called "Let the Children Play" collects used soccer balls and other sports equipment to send  with volunteers to children  in  poor and war affected  countries. For more information  visit  www.ncf.ca/yfocus/childsplay  

b) Brother Clubs. 
Y FOCUS maintains links with several clubs around the world, exchanging bulletins, letters and visits. Occasionally we will join together in development projects and we have the distinction of having sponsored the first club in Sierra Leone, in Freetown. Another important partner club is Nairobi South in Kenya.

c) Youth Educational Exchange Program. 
Children of club members around the world can spend up to a year in the home of a club member in another country. Over the years we have hosted 16 young people from Norway, Denmark, Sweden, Finland and Kenya. At the same time we have sent young people to Denmark, Norway, Sweden, India, Australia and New Zealand. More emphasis is now being placed on the short term program due to difficulties in placing students in schools. 

d) Short Term Exchange Program (STEP) 
This is an international exchange program where young people ages 14 - 25 years, from Y Service clubs or from the Y can spend up to 12 weeks with a family in another country. Up to now we have hosted  STEP students from Finland, Malaysia, Japan, Taiwan and Russia. 

e) Brotherhood Fund 
Club members are eligible to apply for grants to travel to conventions or to visit other clubs in other countries. Most years we host an international visitor travelling as a Brotherhood delegate. Club members host these international visitors in their homes and we usually have a special meeting so that all the club can meet the delegate. We raise money through personal donations at meetings and through the sale of used stamps we collect. Each club must contribute a minimum of $5 per member, per year, to have members eligible for grants. 

f) Alexander Scholarship Fund 
Y Service Clubs in Canada provide annually, about 10 scholarships of at least $500 to YMCA Canada for young people and Y staff for university training or upgrading courses. We collect funds through a Booster Prize raffle at the dinner meetings and through personal donations. 

g) Canadian Friends of Sierra Leone 
After the end of the civil war in Sierra Leone in 2000, there is now a fragile peace that will need years of support. Through various programs we try to raise  much needed funds for the work of  indigenous Sierra Leone NGO's in their peace building work. To facilitate this work the club founded a network called the Canadian Friends of Sierra Leone, working with the Sierra Leone Descendants Union, interested churches and individuals. By the end of 2004, the network had raised over $500 000 for peace building projects and the club continues to support education and training projects there.

g) George Jackson Youth Leadership Development Fund 
We work mainly through the Y in Ottawa and set up in the Y the George Jackson Fund initially to assist international students who are studying in Ottawa, especially those at Carleton University. The fund is a place of last resort when all other sources have been exhausted. Funds are also available to young Canadians for travel or study, to further their experience in and understanding of international issues.
