Y FOCUS - Ottawa Fund Raising
Y FOCUS has established a certain number of annual projects that we can manage but we are always looking for new ideas and are open to assisting other organizations in their fund raising rather than create our own. 

a) Christmas Trees 
This is our largest project using the front of the Metro Y as our sales area. In 1992 we opened a second lot at the Carlingwood Y on Lockhart Ave to support the Britannia Community  House in the west end of Ottawa. Normally we raise between $5000 and $6000 annually. Every club member is asked to take three two hour shifts and help with the set-up and cleanup. We continue to build a list of helpers from among our families and friends. 

b) Sales 
We use the Y lobby for sales at Christmas and Easter, especially for plants. Sometimes there are garage sales or sales of items such as chocolates. In 2006 we  opened a Fair  Trade Boutique called the Fair World Y Boutique, to promote Fair Trade and raise funds for the Y's  Community Fund. We try to staff the boutique with volunteers  during the mornings, from 9 a.m. - 1  p.m. For information about our products click here.

c) Time of Fast 
During February, all Y Service clubs around the world are asked to support development projects of the World Alliance of YMCAs by giving up one or more meals and contributing the money saved. Some of our members fast for 24 hours and get sponsors allowing us to raise from $1000 - $2000 annually. 

d) Supporting our Community Partners 
We try to support those groups with whom we work in the community, especially the National Capital Region  YMCA-YWCA and the Canadian Friends of Sierra Leone, plus Gracefield Conference and Camping Centre, and others. 

e) International Funds of Y Service Clubs. 
We raise funds for several Y Service Club programs by personal contributions and small fund raising events at meetings. We hold draws for the Alexander Scholarship Fund to support Y staff training We collect loose change and stamps for the Brotherhood Fund to help club members and Y staff travel to other clubs and conventions. We accept contributions for the Endowment Fund in honour of deceased  or long-time members. Each year we also allocate funds for work with young people and exchange students within the Central Canada Region. 

f) Fair Trade Certified Sports Balls 
This project began in 2003 as a way to support our work in Sierra Leone and other war-torn countries where sports and education play an important role in building peace. The project is now a national  project of Y Service Clubs and the Ottawa club co-ordinates a Canada-wide network of clubs, Fair Trade  groups and churches to sell Fair Trade sports balls.
