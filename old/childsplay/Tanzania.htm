Here is some information about the country and the particular projects you will be helping with your donations.

The agency we are supporting is the Canadian Support of Rural African Initiatives (CSRAI), based in Toronto.

Information: info@csrai.com

 

CSRAI supports an AIDS orphanage run by the local NGO, MKUKI, in Tanzania. Since the early '90's MKUKI has been providing services and support to those infected and affected by HIV/AIDS in a rural area of Tanzania in the Kilimanjaro Region. MKUKI has provided many programs for orphans, who have lost parents to HIV/AIDS. MKUKI works closely with the schools in their area where principals and teachers identify the orphans in most critical need. Most recently they have started a school lunch program that provides a nutritious noon meal for the orphans at these schools. Soccer balls have been given to Mkyashi Primary School and Kilema Primary School. The combined enrolment at these schools is over 2,000.

"Through this network of schools Mkuki, was able to distribute the soccer balls you donated. This is a huge gift for these children who already have suffered so much in their young lives. This will bring endless enjoyment to so many children who rarely, if ever, see or use a new soccer ball - but love the game of soccer!! Many thanks, from CSRAI, MKUKI and all the students who are benefiting from your donation, warm regards," Bonnie Gray, President,

 

On 26 April 1964, Tanganyika and Zanzibar merged to form a new republic, the name "Tanzania" being adopted on 29 October 1964. Like the name of the country, the new flag was a merger of that of its constituent parts. The lower green stripe of the former Tanganyika flag took the blue of the Zanzibari flag, and the stripes were re-arranged diagonally to give them equal status.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

United Republic of Tanzania at a glance.

President: Jakaya Kikwete (2005)

Prime Minister: Edward Lowassa (2005)

Land area: 342,100 sq mi (886,039 sq km); total area: 364,900 sq mi (945,087 sq km)1

Population (2006 est.): 37,445,392 (growth rate: 1.8%); birth rate: 37.7/1000; infant mortality rate: 96.5/1000; life expectancy: 45.6; density per sq mi: 109

Capital (2003 est.): Dodoma, 164,500; Largest city (2003 est.): Dar es Salaam, 2,489,800

Monetary unit: Tanzanian shilling

Languages: Swahili, English (both official); Arabic; many local languages

Ethnicity/race: native African 99% (includes 95% Bantu, consisting of well over 100 tribes), Asian, European, and Arab 1%

Religions: mainland: Christian 30%, Islam 35%, indigenous 35%; Zanzibar: more than 99% Islam
