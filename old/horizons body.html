Y FOCUS- Ottawa
International Outreach Projects

As part of an international movement, Y FOCUS has a strong interest in what goes on around the
world.  Our current International theme is "Build a Culture of Peace - Say No to Violence"

a) International development.
As our way of supporting the international development work of the YMCA world-wide, our club raises
funds through voluntary contributions and a 24 Hour Fast held in February. This is the month when all 
clubs in Y'sdom International celebrate a Time of Fast. We encourage all members to fast and obtain 
sponsors or at least sponsor another member. The February dinner meeting has an international 
development theme and at least two other dinners are devoted to international topics as part of our 
attempt to educate our members about international issues. Through Time of Fast we have directly 
supported the partnership of the Ottawa-Carleton Y with the Togo YMCA. 

b) The newest project is the importation and sale of Fair Trade Sports Balls partly to raise funds for
peace building projects in war-torn countries but also to draw attention to the potential of Fair Trade 
and the evil of child labour in industries such as hand stitched sports balls. For the latest information 
visit www.ncf.ca/yfocus/fairtrade 

b) Brother Clubs.
Y FOCUS maintains links with several clubs around the world, exchanging bulletins, letters and visits. Occasionally we will join together in development projects and we have the distinction of having 
sponsored the first club in Sierra Leone, in Freetown. Other partner clubs include Naperville, Illinois,
Nairobi South, Kenya, Gdynia, Poland, Freetown ,Sierra Leone and St Petersburg, Russia. Club 
members are invited to act as the club representative to one of our Brother clubs.

c) Youth Educational Exchange Program.
Children of club members around the world can spend up to a year in the home of a club member
in another country. Over the years we have hosted 16 young people from Norway, Denmark, 
Sweden, Finland and Kenya. At the same time we have sent young people to Denmark, Norway, 
Sweden, India, Australia and New Zealand. More emphasis is now being placed on the short term
program due to difficulties in placing students in schools.

d) Short Term Exchange Program (STEP)
This is an international exchange program where young people ages 14 - 25 years, from Y Service
clubs or from the Y can spend up to 12 weeks with a family in another country. Up to now we have
hosted  STEP students from Finland, Malaysia, Japan, Taiwan and Russia.

e) Brotherhood Fund
Club members are eligible to apply for grants to travel to conventions or to visit other clubs in other 
countries. Most years we host an international visitor travelling as a Brotherhood delegate. Club 
members host these international visitors in their homes and we usually have a special meeting so that
all the club can meet the delegate. We raise money through personal donations at meetings and 
through the sale of used stamps we collect. Each club must contribute a minimum of $5 per member, 
per year, to have members eligible for grants.

f) Alexander Scholarship fund
Y Service Clubs in Canada provide annually, about 10 scholarships of at least $500 to YMCA 
Canada for young people and Y staff for university training or upgrading courses. We collect funds 
through a Booster Prize raffle at the dinner meetings and through personal donations.

g) Canadian Friends of Sierra Leone
After the end of the civil war in Sierra Leone in 2000, there is now a fragile peace that will need
years of support. Through various programs we try to raise  much needed funds for the work of 
indigenous Sierra Leone NGO's in their peace building work. To facilitate this work the club 
founded a network called the Canadian Friends of Sierra Leone, working with the Sierra Leone 
Descendants Union, interested churches and individuals.

g) George Jackson Youth Leadership Development Fund
We work mainly through the Y in Ottawa and set up in the Y the George Jackson Fund initially
to assist international students who are studying in Ottawa, especially those at Carleton University. 
The fund is a place of last resort when all other sources have been exhausted. Funds are also 
available to young Canadians to further their experience in and understanding of international issues.
