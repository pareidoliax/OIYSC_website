The U.N. Decade for Non-Violence and a Culture of Peace for Children

 

What is a Culture of Peace?

Based on the ideas behind the U.N. Decade for a Culture of Peace and Non-Violence for the Children of the World, a culture of peace consists of values, attitudes and behaviours that reflect and inspire social interaction and sharing based on the principles of freedom, justice and democracy, human right, tolerance and solidarity.

 

We must reject violence and try to prevent conflicts by tackling their root causes, solve problems through dialogue and negotiations,  guarantee the full exercise of all rights and guarantee the means for people to  fully participate in the development process of their society.

 

 All this can be done by promotion of development, education for peace, the free flow of information and the wider participation of women.

 

           

What are the foundations of a Culture of Peace?   

 

There are SIX THEMES in the Culture of Peace Manifesto outlined below.

 

Respect for life :                        Human rights and equality         - promote understanding

Listen to understand:                 Justice and democracy              - promote freedom

Share with others:                     Economic opportunity               - promote debt relief

Preserve the planet:                   Environmental protection           - promote caring for the earth

Reject violence:                        A culture of peace                    - promote non-violence

Rediscover solidarity:                Community development           - promote democracy

 

Culture of Peace Manifesto

 

A group of Nobel Peace Prize Laureates drafted manifesto 2000 on the occasion of the 50th Anniversary of the Universal Declaration of Human Rights.

 

Because the year 2000 must be a new beginning, an opportunity to transform - all together - the culture of war and violence into a culture of peace and non-violence.

 

Because this transformation demands the participation of each and everyone of us and must offer young people and future generations the values that can inspire them to shape a world based on justice, solidarity, liberty, dignity, harmony and prosperity for all.

 

Because the culture of peace can underpin sustainable development, environmental protection and the well-being of each person.

 

Because I am aware of my share of responsibility for the future of humanity, in particular to the children of today and tomorrow.

 

I pledge in my daily life, in my family, my work, my community, my country and my region to:

 

1. Respect all life. Respect the life and dignity of each human being without discrimination or prejudice.

 

2. Reject violence. Practice non-violence, rejecting violence in all its forms: physical, sexual, psychological, economical and social particularly towards the most deprived and vulnerable, such as children and adolescents.

 

3. Share with others. Share my time and material resources in a spirit of generosity, to put an end to exclusion, injustice and political and economic oppression.

 

4. Listen to understand. Defend freedom of expression and cultural diversity, giving preference always to dialogue and listening without engaging in fanaticism, defamation and the rejection of others.

 

5. Preserve the planet. Promote consumer behaviour that is responsible and development practices that respect all forms of life and preserve the balance of nature on the planet.

 

6. Rediscover solidarity. Contribute to the development of my community, with the full participation of women and respect for democratic principles, in order to create together new forms of solidarity.
