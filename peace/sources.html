Information Sources

SAY NO TO VIOLENCE- BUILD A CULTURE OF PEACE

A listing of web sites, books and videos dealing with important issues around the themes of non-violence and building a culture of peace.The assembly of this information is an ongoing process and this list will be updated regularly. 

The information sources are grouped under the following headings:

Peace issues and conflict resolution

Women’s and children’s rights

School and youth violence

Spousal/child abuse and neglect

War affected children - refugees, street children and child soldiers

Child sexual exploitation - prostitution and pornography

Child labour and sweatshops

Protect the Planet.

Canadian Broadcast Standards Councilhttp://www.cbsc.ca

The Canadian Broadcast Standards Council (CBSC) is anindependent, not?for?profit organization established by theCanadian Association of Broadcasters (CAB). It plays anintermediate role in the regulatory process and promotesself?regulation in programming matters by Canada's privatesector broadcasters.

Site includes information on media violence, children's programming, and rating systems, as well as codes of ethics and codes regarding media violence. There is a full text document on Canada and media violence, including rules for children's programming. Links include those to similar sites around the world, to broadcasting companies, and legal links. 


 

The Empower Programhttp://www.empowered.org/About/about_index.htm

This program works with young people and educators to overcome the all too often accepted notion that violence is a rite of passage.The Empower Program's mission is to work with youth to end the culture of violence. The program trains teachers and youth educators at schools, hospitals, and other unique sites nationwide to implement our award?winning curricula and creates educational partnerships with schools and community?based organizations around the country to build safe and healthy communities while promotingyouth leadership in these efforts through establishing and encouraging youth?driven initiatives; 

The program also advocates for young people's safety through public speaking and outreach and 

teaches youth in the Washington, D.C. metropolitan area how to prevent violence.

8.Protect The Planet


 

UNEPhttp://www.unep.org/

UN Environment Program.  Again more information on environmental issues, excellent reports etc.online.Good info also on World Environment Day (July 5th).


 

Union of Concerned Scientists http://www.ucsusa.org/index.html

Information on environmental issues.
 

The Sierra Club http://www.sierraclub.ca

Founded in 1892 to protect the Sierra Nevada wilderness, the Sierra Club is now concerned with climate change, toxic chemical use, loss of biological diversity and more. It has been active in Canada since 1969 and is noted for its grassroots involvement.
 

World Wildlife Fund http://www.wwf.ca

The worlds largest independent conservation organization, protecting wildlife and habitats around the world through publicity, education and letter writing campaigns.
 

Greenpeace Http://www.greenpeacecanada.org

Formed in 1971 in Vancouver, Greenpeace has over 2 million members world-wide with three major campaigns, genetic engineering, climate change and the protection of forests.
 

David Suzuki Foundation. Http://www.davidsuzuki.org

Established in 1990, the foundation focuses its research mostly on climate change and issues affecting Canada's northwest coast.
 
 

Evergreen Http://www.evergreen.ca

Started as a tree planting program in Toronto, Evergreen has as its mission, bringingnature back to Canada's cities, creating community gardens, providing habitats for birds and butterflies and rehabilitating landscapes all without using pesticides and herbicides.
 

Pollution Probe http://www.pollutionprobe.org

Founded in 1969 by U of T students, Pollution Probe works with others to research the effects of pollution, conducts campaigns to educate the public and to encourage governments to change environmental policies.
 

Western Canada Wilderness Committee http://www.wildrenesscommittee.org

This is Canada's largest membership based wilderness preservation organization, founded in 1980, to preserve the Canadian wilderness through research and education.
