Y Focus - OTTAWA
Community Outreach projects

The club has several ongoing projects in the community. Our general approach is to work in partnership with the National Capital Region YMCA/YWCA and selected community groups, responding to specific requests as we are able.

a) YMCA-YWCA of  the National Capital Region
As a Y Service Club in Ottawa, we offer our support and service primarily to the National Capital Region YMCA/YWCA and we are available when called upon. One regular job is the maintenance
of the garden in front of the Y. In 2000 we completed our pledge of $10,000 over 5 years towards the Building Campaign and we usually provide additional support for such programs as "Partners for Youth" or for specific requests  from Y staff. During the past years we have moved to increase our Y involvement with the Y Camp and Y fund raising projects

b) Interval House.
When Interval House started in Ottawa, we were the first service club to offer our assistance. They know they can come to us if they need financial or human resources. We help organize and support 
the annual  Spring Party at the McNabb Community Centre and sometimes take mothers and 
children on outings. We have also held work parties to clean up the grounds, offered residents a 
place to go for a summer break and paid for some children to go to camp.

c) Habitat for Humanity
Starting in 1999, club members have volunteered in the building of homes organized by Habitat for Humanity, National Capital Region. Specifically we do the landscaping work for the homes.

d) Other community groups.
The club responds to requests for help from various groups, especially those with whom we have worked closely over the years, including the United Way, Gracefield Camp and Conference Centre. 

Projects we consider for support are suggested by our members and we also encourage and support the community involvement of individual members.
